Hey, anyone else notice the inherent tension between "professionalism" and the unbridled joy of creation? I'm being so very disciplined right now about not changing the URL to something silly. It's funny that, including and maybe especially in the professional world, I've gotten hired off the unbridled joy of creation so much more often.

On a related note, anyone else feel a sort of anxiety about putting their real name on things? It's gotten a little better now that I'm mostly "Ness" and not "Vanessa", but I still don't like seeing my full name written out anywhere except, I don't know, government forms and their ilk. Maybe it's Gen Z's instinctual fear of putting all of our identity into one spot. I prefer to be dispersed on the Internet, a disembodied pollen of cryptic usernames and abstract thumbnails drifting across Discord servers and subreddits and quiz sites from 2017.

But I guess this is what being an adult means. Time to lay my full name, and my partial self, on the operating table or the altar called the Internet. Eat well, search engines.
