Hello, World!

Anyone else notice the inherent tension between "professionalism" and the unbridled joy of creation? Funny that - including and maybe especially in the professional world - I've gotten hired off the unbridled joy of creation so much more often. Much to think about on this silly little demo website. I'm being so very disciplined right now about not changing the URL to something silly.

On a related note, anyone else feel a sort of anxiety about putting their full name on things? It's gotten a little better that I'm "Ness" and not "Vanessa" - "Vanessa" still always sounds to me like a sticky little five-year-old - but I still don't like seeing my full name written out. Maybe it's Gen Z's instinctual fear of putting all of our identity into one spot. I prefer to be dispersed on the Internet, disembodied, a pollen of cryptic usernames and abstract thumbnails drifting across Discord servers and subreddits and abandoned quiz sites from 2017. Or maybe it's because it's a part of my identity I can't really control.

But I guess this is what being an adult means. Time to lay my full name, and my partial self, on the operating table or the altar called the Internet. Eat well, search engines.
